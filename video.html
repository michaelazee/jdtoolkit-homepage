Reflection: The Face Behind Mulan

“I think one of the reasons why I got into acting is to have a positive impact on speaking about our personal journeys, or making connections. It’s wonderful when you can make that kind-of lifelong connection with someone.”

Ming-Na Wen, my mother, has been a professional actor for over three decades. She has starred in multiple films and television series, including The Joy Luck Club and ER, and recently received the Disney Legends Award in 2019. This piece explores her experiences when voicing Disney’s Mulan in the '90s, and how this character of Asian descent has affected both her and countless others around the world when it comes to embracing one’s identity.

My mom called me from my home in Calabasas, California. We have Facetimed often during this pandemic while I'm working in NYC. This interview was an honor to film since I got to learn more about my mom and her work.
